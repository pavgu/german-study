# Definition Rules

Use these rules when a deck needs a short German explanation field rather than a full sentence.

## Core Rules

- Write the definition in simple, natural German.
- Prefer a short paraphrase over a dictionary-style gloss.
- Keep it compact, usually about 3 to 10 words.
- Target one meaning only.
- Make the wording learner-friendly, ideally around B1-B2 comprehension level unless the target word itself is advanced.

## What To Avoid

- Do not include the answer word itself.
- Do not define a word only as the negation of its opposite.
- Do not turn the definition into a cloze sentence or mini exercise.
- Do not add a separate example sentence unless the consuming prompt explicitly asks for one.
- Do not over-explain morphology, register, or grammar if a meaning-based paraphrase is enough.

## Good Patterns

- synonym-like paraphrase
- short plain-German explanation
- usage-based meaning cue

## Examples

- `laufen` -> `sich schnell zu Fuß bewegen`
- `implizit` -> `nicht direkt gesagt, aber gemeint`
- `zulässig` -> `nach den Regeln erlaubt`
